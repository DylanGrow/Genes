# Statistics Dashboard Guide

Visualize your family history with beautiful charts and comprehensive analytics.

## Overview

The Statistics Dashboard provides data-driven insights into your family tree with:
- 📊 **Interactive Charts** - Visual representations of family data
- 📈 **Key Metrics** - Important statistics at a glance
- 🎯 **Detailed Analysis** - Deep dive into family patterns
- 🌍 **Geographic Insights** - Migration and location patterns
- ⏳ **Temporal Analysis** - Births, deaths, and lifespans over time

## Accessing Statistics

1. Click **"Statistics"** in the navigation menu
2. Dashboard loads automatically with current data
3. Updates automatically when you edit data
4. Scroll to see all charts and metrics

## Understanding the Data

### Key Metrics Explained

**Total People**
- Every individual in your tree
- Includes living and deceased
- Privacy-protected living persons

**Living vs Deceased**
- Living: Born < 100 years ago, no death date
- Deceased: Has death date OR born > 100 years ago
- Privacy automatically applied

**Generations**
- Calculated from root person upward
- Counts parent-child relationships
- Maximum depth of tree

**Average Lifespan**
- Only includes deceased with known dates
- Calculated from birth to death
- Excludes living persons
- Filters out invalid ages (>150 years)

**Average Children per Family**
- Families with at least one child
- Mean number of children
- Fertility patterns

### Chart Interpretations

**Gender Distribution**
- Balanced: ~50/50 male/female
- Skewed: May indicate incomplete data
- Unknown: Missing gender information

**Births by Decade**
- Peaks: Baby booms, family growth
- Valleys: Wars, migrations, data gaps
- Trends: Population changes over time

**Lifespan by Gender**
- Historical: Women often lived longer
- Modern: Gap narrowing
- Variations: Healthcare, lifestyle

**Surnames**
- Dominant names: Main family lines
- Rare names: Married-in families
- Distribution: Family diversity

**Birth Places**
- Clusters: Family origins
- Spread: Migration patterns
- Changes: Geographic mobility

## Troubleshooting

### "No data to display"
- **Cause:** Empty or minimal tree
- **Solution:** Add more people and data

### Charts look empty
- **Cause:** Missing dates or information
- **Solution:** Fill in birth/death dates

### Incorrect statistics
- **Cause:** Data entry errors
- **Solution:** Use validation tools to find errors

### Lifespan seems wrong
- **Cause:** Invalid dates
- **Solution:** Check date validation report

### Missing people in charts
- **Cause:** Privacy filter or missing data
- **Solution:** Add death dates or wait 100 years

## FAQ

**Q: How often do statistics update?**
A: Automatically when you view the Statistics tab or after editing data.

**Q: Can I export charts?**
A: Not yet, but coming soon. Currently, take screenshots.

**Q: Why are some people missing from charts?**
A: Living persons are privacy-protected. Add death dates to include them.

**Q: How is average lifespan calculated?**
A: Only deceased persons with both birth and death dates, excluding ages >150 years.

**Q: Can I customize chart colors?**
A: Not yet, but customization is planned for future updates.

**Q: Do statistics include living persons?**
A: Yes in counts, but not in lifespan calculations or detailed analysis.

**Q: How accurate are the statistics?**
A: As accurate as your data. Use validation tools to improve accuracy.

**Q: Can I see statistics for specific branches?**
A: Not yet, but branch-specific analysis is planned.
